About Me

I’m an Assistant Professor at Princeton University with appointments in the Department of Computer Science and the School of Public and International Affairs...

I research topics at the intersection of language and decision-making, particularly in legal contexts...

I’m particularly interested in:

  • language-grounded reinforcement learning, alignment, and strategic decision-making
  • public interest artificial intelligence
  • creating a better science of AI evaluations
  • leveraging, understanding, and evolving the law to steer toward positive outcomes for AI

Previously, I received a JD from Stanford Law School where I was advised by Dan Ho and Mariano-Flores Cuéllar...

I also received my PhD in Computer Science (AI) at Stanford University...
